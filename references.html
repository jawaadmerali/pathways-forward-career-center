References

Scholarly and professional sources cited throughout this site (APA 7th edition)

The content, framework, assessment guidance, and equity considerations on this site are informed by the following research and professional standards. In-text citations appear on relevant pages throughout the site.

Reference List

  1. Bandura, A. (1997). Self-efficacy: The exercise of control. W. H. Freeman.
  2. Engle, J. P., & Tinto, V. (2008). Moving beyond access: College success for low-income, first-generation students. Pell Institute for the Study of Opportunity in Higher Education.
  3. Holland, J. L. (1997). Making vocational choices: A theory of vocational personalities and work environments (3rd ed.). Psychological Assessment Resources.
  4. Lent, R. W., Brown, S. D., & Hackett, G. (1994). Toward a unifying social cognitive theory of career and academic interest, choice, and performance. Journal of Vocational Behavior, 45(1), 79–122. https://doi.org/10.1006/jvbe.1994.1027
  5. Lent, R. W., Brown, S. D., & Hackett, G. (2000). Contextual supports and barriers to career choice: A social cognitive analysis. Journal of Counseling Psychology, 47(1), 36–49. https://doi.org/10.1037/0022-0167.47.1.36
  6. National Career Development Association. (2015). 2015 NCDA code of ethics. https://www.ncda.org/aws/NCDA/asset_manager/get_file/3395?ver=483748
  7. Super, D. E. (1990). A life-span, life-space approach to career development. In D. Brown & L. Brooks (Eds.), Career choice and development (2nd ed., pp. 197–261). Jossey-Bass.

How Sources Inform This Site

Theory Integration

Lent et al. (1994, 2000) provide the Social Cognitive Career Theory framework used throughout the site. Super (1990) informs our developmental timelines and normalization of career exploration. Bandura (1997) supports our emphasis on building self-efficacy through experience.

Assessment Guidance

Holland (1997) RIASEC theory underpins the O*NET Interest Profiler. NCDA (2015) ethical standards guide our presentation of assessment purpose, limitations, interpretation, and ethical use.

Equity Considerations

Engle & Tinto (2008) inform our focus on first-generation students and financial barriers. Lent et al. (2000) contextual supports and barriers model structures our equity section's approach to systemic obstacles.
