Equity & Access

Barriers are real. This site helps address them.

Our Commitment

Career development is not a level playing field. This site provides information and connections students might not otherwise have (Lent et al., 2000; Engle & Tinto, 2008).

First-Generation Students

First-gen students often lack "college knowledge" that peers from college-educated families absorb at home. This site offers plain-language guides, timelines, and free resources.

Financial Barriers

  • Compare net price, not sticker price
  • Consider community college and apprenticeships
  • Use fee waivers for tests and applications
  • FAFSA is always free at StudentAid.gov

Underrepresented Students

Barriers like stereotypes and lack of representation are environmental, not personal deficits. Seek mentors and communities who share your background.

Still Exploring?

Uncertainty at your age is normal (Super, 1990). Try low-commitment experiences like job shadowing. Community college lets you explore at lower cost. Your first choice is not your forever choice.
