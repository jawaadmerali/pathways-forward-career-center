Your Future Starts with Informed Choices

Pathways Forward is a professional online career resource center designed for first-generation high school juniors and seniors who are preparing for life after graduation. Whether you are exploring college, workforce training, military service, or still figuring things out — this site is built for you.

Who This Site Is For

Students ages 16–18, especially first-generation and underserved learners navigating post-secondary transitions without family experience to guide them.

Why This Site Exists

To provide research-informed career exploration, self-discovery tools, and actionable guidance so you can make confident, equitable decisions about your future.

How to Use This Site

Start with the Framework to understand our approach, take an assessment, explore pathways, then use the Action Plan to take your next steps. Visit Equity & Access for support resources.

Our Population & Professional Context

Pathways Forward serves first-generation high school juniors and seniors (ages 16–18) in public secondary school settings. These students are at a critical developmental transition: moving from adolescence toward young adulthood while making decisions about education, training, and career that will shape their futures (Super, 1990).

Many first-generation students face unique challenges — limited access to college knowledge within their families, financial uncertainty, and fewer professional networks (Engle & Tinto, 2008). They may also experience lower career self-efficacy, meaning they doubt their ability to succeed in certain fields (Lent et al., 2000). This site is designed within a school counseling and student affairs context, complementing the work of counselors, teachers, and community partners who support equitable post-secondary access.

Developmental & Transitional Needs

At this stage, students need to develop career identity, build self-efficacy, understand available pathways, and create concrete action plans — all while managing academic demands and social-emotional growth. This site addresses those needs through theory-informed tools, curated resources, and step-by-step guidance.
